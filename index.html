What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is an artificial intelligence chatbot created by OpenAI that uses natural language processing to create humanlike conversational dialogue. The language model can respond to questions and compose various written content, including articles, social media posts, and essays.

ChatGPT uses deep learning, a subset of machine learning, to produce humanlike text through transformer neural networks. The transformer predicts text including the next word, sentence, or paragraph based on its training data's typical sequence.

My Product - "CheatsGPT"

I decided I wanted to focus on a project that sees Technology attempting to fix a problem caused by Technology. As my partner works for our local university, ChatGPT has been a hot topic of discussion in our household and I felt it would make for an interesting thought experiment.

Counteracting Human Educational Abuse of Technological Systems, or CheatsGPT abbreviated, is an adaptive A.I comparable in scope to ChatGPT, designed to check assignment submissions across the globe for similarities with articles / essays produced by ChatGPT.

CheatsGPT is achieved as follows:

  • Stage 1: Form a database of essays created by responses from ChatGPT using thousands of common keywords as prompts and human written essays as a baseline
  • Stage 2: Compare the uploaded student essay with the database
  • Stage 3: Calculate the Probability of Plagiarism (PoP) index - a percentage chance that the essay was produced by ChatGPT based on sentence structure, word usage, grammar, and content relevance

CheatsGPT subsequently uses uploaded essays to self-improve and learn the difference between A.I and human written essays, furthering its abilities to detect A.I Plagiarism.

Initial market research shows a similar product currently exists called ZeroGPT, however with one major difference; ZeroGPT is limited by its original programming with no ability to learn or adapt.

Who would benefit?

Education professionals across schools, colleges, and universities would benefit greatly from being able to decipher whether material has been created by students or not. However, I truly believe that essay writing and the reflective nature of demonstrating knowledge gained from studying is fundamental to learning, so ultimately those who would benefit most would be students themselves. An over-reliance on programs like ChatGPT will inevitably decrease knowledge retention and potentially create generations of graduates with unlearned skills.

Simply knowing a tool like CheatsGPT exists and is being widely used by universities could deter students from considering the use of A.I to skip core subject matter.

Why would it fail?

ChatGPT took 5 years to develop using the shell of the original program GPT3. This was a significant investment in time and resources and it had some brilliant minds working on it. One could therefore argue whether another program of its scale could even be created without the original members of OpenAI who worked on it.

Technology is forever advancing and as demonstrated with Viruses and Antiviruses there is a near constant arms race to make the next product to beat the last. Without constant development and updates, CheatsGPT will eventually become obsolete as the next super A.I is released.

And ultimately, Cheats GPT is not able to provide definitive proof of plagiarism. Even with an incredibly high probability of a paper being matched on the A.I database, there would still be a chance (however slim) that a student could have written the essay themselves.
